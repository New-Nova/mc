Getting started

New Nova is a towny 1.21.3 minecraft server based on the discontinued nova map from EarthMC. This guide will get you started on getting started yet again on nova. Join using the address newnova.blockworlds.io, join our Discord server. We are gonna guess if you're joining New Nova you understand the basics of towny or have played EarthMC before. So here's some information if you owned a town in the previous EarthMC map. Your town will be there; however, if you have changed your username, you will need to contact a staff member to get your town back. The town spawns and nation spawns have also been reset to a random point in your town. Make sure to run /t set spawn and /n set spawn to set your town and nation spawn to somewhere else. If you have any other questions, feel free to ask in the Discord server.

Dynmap

The interactive web map can be found at http://newnova.blockworlds.io:8123. This is a great tool to navigate the map. A head icon with your name and skin marks your location on the server. If you can't find yourself on the map, make sure to check your coordinates in-game and compare them to coordinates displayed on the map. Below the coordinates, you will find a menu where territory borders can be toggled. Move the mouse to the right of the screen to open a larger black menu. Here you can see who is online.

Creating a town

In order to create a town, you need 64 gold in your inventory. Make sure your stand in the area you want to settle your town in before running the command. Once you're ready, open chat and type:

/t new <Town_Name_Here>

Make sure your town name does not include a space or any special characters. Towns are safe from raiding and looting. Towns can only be raided during war only if they fall into ruin when deleted. Mayors (the player that created the town) can toggle taxes, pvp, etc. For more info, do:

/t toggle

In order to expand your town one chunk, you have to pay 16 gold (Note: The more chunks you claim the more gold you have to pay.) and run:

/t claim

This command will let you add town residents:

/t add <Player_Name_Here>

All towny commands are listed on the official towny website, here. In doubt, run:

/t ?

Towns where mayors haven't ever joined the server get randomly chosen to get deleted daily. Once you join the server once, your town is safe from deletion.

Joining a nation

A nation is a collection of towns with one town as the capital. The town which created the nation is set as capital by default. Your town will get several benefits by joining a nearby nation. You will have access to teleport to the capital where much of the nation's trade is done. You also have access to other capitals your nation is allied to. In order to join a nation your town has to be within 3000 blocks of the capital (you can measure the distance on the dynmap).

To create your own nation you have to pay 1024 gold from your town vault. Your town will become the nation's capital and town mayor will become nation king. Make sure you name your nation after the IRL country where it is located on the map. You can also name it after past nations. You can name your nation fictional names just avoid using special characters and follow the rules.

/n new <Nation_Name_Here>

Ranks

You can set different ranks for your town/nation members. This gives you the power to limit access, but also grant your residents different capabilities.

/town rank add <Player_Name Rank_Name> or /nation rank add <Player_Name Rank_Name>

  • Town ranks
  • Assistant - Has almost all of the commands the Mayor has.
  • Trusted - A rank that has all private plot permissions everywhere in the town.
  • Donator - Can deposit gold into the town bank.
  • Sheriff - Is able to jail other town members and jail outlaws.
  • Helper - Has some commands that the mayor has, similar to Assistant
  • VIP - Pays no taxes.

  • Nation ranks
  • Assistant - Has almost all of the commands the King has.
  • Helper - Has some commands that the king has.

  • Server ranks
  • Premium supporters - Awesome people who pay the bills every month. They have golden colored usernames.
  • Helper, Mod, Admin, Owner - Server staff, moderators deal with player issues and support while admins and owners deal with technical issues. Helpers assist moderators in overseeing the community.
